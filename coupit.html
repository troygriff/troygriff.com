Coupit

User Experience Design

Overview

Background

The Coupit mobile app was our final project for a user-centered design class instructed by Paula Bauch at Microsoft Design. I was part of a team that designed a mobile application to help save money by finding deals from local restaurants and stores. Throughout the project I applied user research methods, visual and interaction design skills, methods for evaluating designs, and skills for communicating my designs.

Skills

  • User Research
  • Heuristics Analysis
  • Persona Creation
  • Usability Testing
  • Interactive Prototyping

    • Process

    User Research

    Our team began the project by conducting semi-structured interviews with classmates to gain a better understanding of our audience needs. User interviews demonstrated a need for wanting to save money when they make purchases, especially in an electronic format, for example:

  • “I don’t use paper coupons because everything is electronic now.”
  • “I wish there was a way to become more conscious about spending money on food.”
  • “I like to share everything with my friends online.”

    • Defining User Needs

    Our user research results showed that our team needed to focus our design efforts around three main user needs:

  • Connectivity (Social networks, review systems, email businesses)
  • Ease of Use (Accessibility needs, intuitiveness, ease of use)
  • Practicality (Simple design, usefulness, scanning ability, looking towards the future with payment systems)

    • Personas

    Personas were created to allow empathy towards our users and have a point of reference for future design decisions to be made.

    USABILITY TESTING & DESIGN DECISIONS


    Lack of Color

    60% of testers said that the application lacked color and seemed too simple. With this feedback, a background has been implemented to make the application “colorful and easier on the eyes.”

    Search Functions

    In early prototypes, the search function appeared on a variety of pages, but four testers said that it’d be “easier to search and access the searching functions if they were in a more localized area.” Revisions included search features only on pages that require a search function, to increase user task completion times.

    Settings Panel

    A more extensive settings panel was created because all testers stated that they want privacy when roaming an application, which brought forth the two extra setting options of “Connect to Facebook” and “Connect to Twitter.” This will help them control their content if they do not like things being posted or having their activities seen by other people.

    Review System

    Internal team critiques demonstrated review pages can become cluttered. To solve this, our team designed a modal to appear when a user wants to write a review, making it easier for the user to write their review without needing to scroll all the way to the bottom of the reviews to write their message.
