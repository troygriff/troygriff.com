Orbit

MOBILE OS DESIGN

Overview

Background

Orbit is a project I worked on as a part of a team in a mobile user interface design class. I helped design a new mobile experience from the ground up. This included establishing an overall design language, gestures and haptics, user interface (UI) frameworks, and detailing how the core "pre-loaded" applications function. The class was unique because being in multidisciplinary teams required communication of our design ideas to work with other groups. At the end of each design sprint, ideas were presented and critiqued in front of our class, following a scrum methodology.

    Concept

    The Orbit operating system is built upon the idea of designing for accessible and intuitive screen usage. Heatmaps of optimal touch points on mobile devices inspired change from the traditional tile layout into a curvature of icons--resembling a planetary orbit.

    Style Guide

    The purpose of our style guide was to establish an overall brand strategy that conveys the uniqueness of the design language. We created a reusable visual toolkit that provided the resources to design multiple apps with a consistent look and feel. The final style guide included color palette, typography guidelines, readability best practices, image guidelines, iconography guidelines, button styles, and badge styling. The Orbit style strategy was heavily influenced by 1970â€™s design with muted colors palettes, thin design elements, and proxima nova font.

    UX Requirements & Personas

    This area focused on overall user experience principles and best practices that will be used across multiple apps. We defined the target users contexts, typical behaviors and needs by developing personas and UX requirements for future design work.

    UI Patterns

    This area focused on reusable UI Patterns that are essential to build out a core set of mobile apps. A small Pattern Library was created that features information about navigation, tabs, sub navigation, navigation bars, breadcrumbs, and pagination.

    Sketches

    Ideas were generated through slow refinement of sketches and rapid prototyping. As our team received feedback in critique sessions, our designs progressed from whiteboards and sketch paper to high fidelity prototypes with software like Adobe Illustrator and Sketch.

    Final Deliverables

    As a final deliverable for the project, I worked with a team to develop utlity applications that adhered to the Orbit theme. Applications such as a wallet, calculator, clock, and weather were designed and submitted and reviewed by mobile designers in the Seattle area.
