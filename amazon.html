Amazon

UX Research

Overview

Background

The Amazon Warehouse Deals service had been using the same website design since its launch, and the user experience for visitors of the Warehouse Deals storefront was in need of improvement. Last summer I was part of a project chartered to determine how users get to the Warehouse Deals site, how they use it, and why.

Skills

  • Competitive Analysis
  • Task Analysis
  • Persona Creation
  • Surveys
  • Usability Scripts
  • Interviews
  • Data Analysis

    • Purpose

    Increase customer conversion by:

  • Finding pathways visitors used to reach the storefront
  • Identifying usability issues with the Warehouse Deals storefront and its discoverability
  • Quantitatively analyzing Amazon Warehouse Deals customers

    • Process

    Competitve Analysis

    The project began by conducting a competitive analysis that studied how other similar organizations handled pre-owned and sale items. Our team analyzed and compiled data about the effectiveness of competitors’ features, allowing us to begin to understand how we fit into the market.

    Survey Creation

    Next, a survey was created that funneled potential participants based upon responses such as age, gender, region, knowledge of Amazon products, and use pattern. This allowed us to get an even distribution between target audiences.

    Usability Scripts

    As the survey responses arrived, our team was hard at work crafting a usability study. We noticed two main user flows through the product listing page and deals section. We designed our script to take the users down these paths and ask them questions along the way.

    Usability Testing

    We then carried out user studies remotely using screen sharing software. We measured metrics such as click rate, time to completion, and wrong pathways allowed us to erical data supporting our claims. Qualitative data such as user responses gave us insight into the thoughts and feelings of the user.

    Result

    Our team analyzed the research data, made design recommendations, and presented our findings to Amazon. Data we collected indicated severe issues with page navigation, layout, and taxonomy. Our team’s recommendations are currently being incorporated into the Warehouse Deals customer experience.
